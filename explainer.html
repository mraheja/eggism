This page documents the technical model used to simulate hydrostatic equilibrium on non-spherical bodies.

Gravity Model (Discrete Volume Summation)

The planet's gravitational field is calculated using a Stack of 20 internal masses. These points are distributed linearly from the South Pole (-Radius) to the North Pole (+Radius) along the planet's primary axis.

To ensure accuracy for different planetary shapes, their separation is multiplied by the Egg Taper factorâ€”shrinking them toward a singular point for spheres and stretching them horizontally matches the asymmetric volume as the planet elongates. Each mass slice is dynamically scaled proportional to the local cross-sectional volume (M ∝ R2).

This approximates the self-pull of a uniform solid body. For spherical worlds (Taper = 0), these slices analyticaly collapse into a single central point mass.

The Gravity Rod Model

Because accurate 3D volume integration is mathematically expensive, this simulation uses a "Needle" approximation. Instead of spreading the mass in all directions (like a solid basketball), we stack it strictly along the vertical axis (like a thin rod).

  • Needle Physics: Gravity is concentrated on a single line. This results in extreme attraction at the polar tips relative to the equator, creating the "deep bulbs" seen at high taper.
  • Solid Planet Reality: A real egg-shaped world would have mass distributed throughout its 3D volume. Its gravity field would be much smoother and more spherical, as the width of the planet would "cushion" the pull and connect the polar seas into a global ocean.

Equipotential Logic

The "sea level" is an Equipotential Surface. Water settles precisely where the sum of gravitational and centrifugal potentials is equal across common radii.

For highly tapered eggs, expect water to congregation primarily at the deep/fat base but also in a separate bulb at the tapered end. This happens because the poles of the planet act like unique centers of attraction, leaving the equator of the long egg high and dry.

Simulation Results

Simulation State Physical Outcome
Taper = 0 Perfectly uniform spherical coverage.
Moderate Taper The ocean retains an egg-like shape, but settling is concentrated globally near the fat base (the area of greatest gravitational volume).
High Taper (Taper > 0.4) Dual accumulation: Primary pooling at the deep base and secondary bulbing at the tapered tip, leaving a dry equatorial band.
High Rotation Equatorial bulge and detached ring formation (Roche Spillover).

Technical Limitations

  • No Self-Gravity: The sea level is determined by the solid planet's mass, ignoring the gravitational attraction of the water body itself.
  • Static Resolution: Uses fixed 20-pole summation; higher densities required for engineering accuracy.
  • Vibrational Equilibrium: Does not simulate acoustic or tidal waves.
