How the Egg Planet Works

This is a real-time 3D visualization of the physics governing land and sea distribution on an egg-shaped world.

Equipotential Physics

The water surface isn't just a sphere—it's calculated using a Gravitational Potential model. This ensures that water physically settles where the potential energy is equal, just like on Earth.

The "Egg" Shape: Gravity is modeled by two internal mass points. One heavy mass at the bottom creates the wide base, and a lighter one at the top creates the tapered end.

The "Ring Effect" (Spillover)

When the planet spins fast enough, a Centrifugal Force fights against gravity. At the equator, gravity is weakest, making it the highest part of the "Gravity Hill."

If you raise the sea level too high, the water spills over the peak and creates a Detached Accretion Disk—a floating ring of ice and water trapped in orbit.

Limitations

  • No Self-Gravity: The ocean obeys the planet's gravity, but doesn't attract itself.
  • Static Fluids: This models hydrostatic equilibrium (where water settles), ignoring waves, tides, and currents.
